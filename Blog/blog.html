"I’m one of those people who has often been really depressed on her birthday. Growing up in a dirt poor, dysfunctional home, my birthdays weren’t exactly great. My mom long had a habit of doing whatever she wanted to do, and often, she’d put in a whole lot of effort but it wasn’t usually about me and what I wanted at all....
Obviously, my negative birthday experiences impacted me and the way I feel about celebrating my own daughter’s birthday. It’s important to me to not repeat the same cycles that made me feel terrible growing up. And it’s important to me that my kid grows up with plenty of great memories she can hold onto.
I want her to feel loved, worth celebrating, and really secure in our bond. There’ts a lot that I can’t control in this world — I can’t help how I was raised, or the fact that her dad isn’t very active in her life.
But as long as I have a choice, I want to do everything I can to make her birthdays special. That’s why we have a party every single year. Even before I got back on my feet after the pregnancy, I decided that fabulous birthday parties were going to be a priority in my life as a single mother. I’d set aside money throughout the year, shop sales — anything to make it happen. Thanks to Jashn I was able to fulfill my this dream."
